# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo Kick streamers running live chat giveaways during a broadcast. They typically keep kickaway.win open on a second monitor or alt-tab back to it between segments. They need to connect a channel, start collection, monitor entrants, and draw winners without breaking flow or losing viewer trust.

## Product Purpose

kickaway.win is a free Kick.com giveaway tool. Streamers connect their channel, collect entrants from live chat, and draw winners with wheel or slot animations. An OBS overlay lets viewers follow the draw on stream. Success means the streamer can run a fair giveaway in seconds, viewers see a transparent and entertaining draw, and the host retains full control over filters, multi-winner rounds, and session settings.

## Brand Personality

Playful live energy with broadcast utility underneath. The interface should feel like part of the show: confetti, draw animations, and neon Kick green for moments that matter. Day-to-day controls stay dense and scannable so a solo streamer can operate mid-stream without hunting for actions. Voice is direct and streamer-native, not corporate SaaS.

Three words: **energetic**, **trustworthy**, **fast**.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing patterns: cream backgrounds, purple gradients, hero metrics, identical icon-card grids, eyebrow kickers on every section.
- Twitch-clone aesthetics: purple-heavy palettes and "yet another streaming tool" familiarity.
- Overdesigned UI: glassmorphism, gradient text, excessive motion, decorative chrome that slows operation.
- AI-slop streaming-tool clichés that prioritize looks over live workflow.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed is the feature.** Every screen should minimize steps from "I'm live" to "winner drawn." Controls belong where the streamer's eyes already are during a giveaway.
2. **Show the draw, earn the trust.** Overlay and animation are not decoration; they prove fairness to chat. The control panel and the viewer-facing moment must stay in sync.
3. **Playful at the climax, utilitarian everywhere else.** Confetti, wheel spin, and neon accent belong to draw moments and live status. Settings, lists, and navigation stay compact and quiet.
4. **Kick-native, not Kick-clone.** Dark canvas and `#53FC18` accent anchor brand recognition without copying Kick.com layout wholesale.
5. **Density with clarity.** Streamers scan lists (entrants, winners, filters) under time pressure. Hierarchy through weight and contrast, not empty space.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Sensible defaults: readable contrast on dark surfaces, keyboard-accessible controls, and reduced-motion support for draw animations (already partially implemented). No formal WCAG certification target beyond standard good practice.
